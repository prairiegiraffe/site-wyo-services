Equipment & Capabilities

Iron Built for Basin Work

WYO Services maintains a diverse fleet of heavy equipment sized for oilfield site development, earthwork, and reclamation across the Powder River Basin.

Our Fleet

Equipment Matched to the Job

Every project in the oilfield has different demands — pad construction requires different iron than reclamation or pipeline work. WYO Services runs the equipment needed to handle each phase of site development, from initial ground-breaking through final restoration.

Our fleet is staged in Gillette and Douglas, which means fast mobilization across the Powder River Basin. No waiting on out-of-state equipment transfers. When work starts, we're ready to roll.

Equipment Categories

What We Run

Dozers

The backbone of pad construction. Dozers handle rough grading, stripping topsoil, pushing material into cut-and-fill positions, and clearing access roads. Essential for every dirt-moving job in the basin.

Excavators

Used for trenching, drainage installation, foundation work, and loading trucks. Excavators are critical for pipeline crossings, cellar digs, and any precision earthwork where reach and bucket control matter.

Motor Graders

Precision finish grading for pad surfaces, road maintenance, and drainage slopes. Motor graders put the final grade on a pad to meet engineered tolerances and keep access roads in working condition through heavy use.

Scrapers

High-volume material movers for large cut-and-fill operations. Scrapers strip, haul, and spread dirt efficiently across pad sites, reducing the number of truck loads needed and keeping material moving on big earthwork projects.

Compactors

Proper compaction is what separates a pad that holds up from one that doesn't. Rollers and compactors densify fill material to spec, ensuring stable surfaces for drilling rigs, tanks, and heavy equipment traffic.

Water Trucks

Dust suppression and moisture conditioning during earthwork. Water trucks keep material at optimal moisture for compaction and control dust on active sites and haul roads — a constant requirement on Powder River Basin projects.

Lowboy & Transport

Moving heavy equipment between sites requires the right hauling capacity. Lowboy trailers transport dozers, excavators, and other iron to and from jobsites across the basin, keeping mobilization tight and on schedule.

Welding & Fabrication

Field welding rigs and shop fabrication capacity for cattle guards, custom steel work, and on-site repairs. WYO Services builds cattle guards in-house and handles structural welding for containment and infrastructure projects.

Fabrication

Steel Work & Custom Builds

WYO Services doesn't just operate heavy equipment — the company fabricates custom steel components for oilfield and ranch applications. Cattle guards are built in-house to handle the weight of oilfield truck traffic while keeping livestock contained. Each guard is fabricated to match road width and load requirements.

Beyond cattle guards, the fabrication shop supports custom containment structures, repair work on heavy equipment, and field welding for site infrastructure. Having fabrication in-house means faster turnaround and tighter quality control on every piece that goes into the ground.

Right-Sized for the Work

Matching Equipment to Every Phase

Different phases of site development demand different machines. WYO Services matches equipment to the scope — whether that's a single dozer on a small reclamation job or a full spread of iron on a multi-well pad build. The fleet is maintained, operated by experienced crews, and staged locally so there's no wasted time getting the right machine to the right site.
