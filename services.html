Our Services

Seven Services. One Contractor.

WYO Services covers every phase of oilfield site work — from initial pad construction through final reclamation and fencing. One crew, one call, one schedule.

Full-Scope Capabilities

What WYO Services Delivers

Each of these services can be contracted individually or bundled into a turnkey package. Either way, you get the same crew, the same standards, and the same accountability.

Pad Construction & Dirt Work

Site grading, cut-and-fill, road building, and pad preparation for drilling and production facilities across the Powder River Basin.

Reclamation & Seeding

Restoring disturbed land to productive use through recontouring, topsoil replacement, erosion control, and native seed establishment.

Fencing & Containment

Perimeter fencing, livestock containment, and site security for active well sites, tank batteries, and reclaimed locations.

Cattle Guards: Fabrication & Installation

Custom-fabricated cattle guards designed for heavy oilfield traffic, built and installed to manage livestock movement at road crossings.

Site Infrastructure Support

Access roads, drainage systems, erosion control, and support structures that keep your operations running without downtime.

Turnkey Site Development

Complete project delivery — from mobilization through final inspection. One contractor, one contract, one timeline for the entire site lifecycle.

Emerging Pipeline Work

Expanding into pipeline support services — right-of-way preparation, trenching support, and pipeline corridor restoration across Wyoming.
The WYO Advantage

Why Work With One Contractor?

Coordinating multiple subcontractors means multiple schedules, multiple points of contact, and multiple chances for things to fall through the cracks. WYO Services eliminates that complexity by handling every phase of site work under one contract.

Your project manager talks to one person. Your timeline has one schedule. And when something needs to change in the field — and it always does — we adjust without the back-and-forth between separate crews. That's how projects stay on track in the Powder River Basin.
